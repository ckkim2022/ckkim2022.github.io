About XPRTIS INSPIRE Program

XPRTIS INSPIRE Program offers high school students interested in software engineering, data science, robotics, design, and business the chance to contribute practically to real-world problem-solving through select projects from our ongoing portfolio. Each year, we carefully interview and select students based on their skills, personality, and motivation. Those chosen will have the opportunity to collaborate with prestigious enterprises and institutions like Chiels (a Samsung subsidiary), as well as research laboratories at UC Berkeley and Carnegie Mellon. Additionally, they'll work alongside global organizations such as the Red Cross.

Every year, we interview and select qualified students based on their skillsets, personality, and motivation. Selected students will be given the opportunity to collaborate not only with renowned enterprises and institutions such as the subsidiary of Samsung and research laboratories of UC Berkeley and Carnegie Mellon, but also with global organizations such as the Red Cross.

  • Application Closed
  • Completed

Project XR, in collaboration with the GIST VR Project at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute, strives to revolutionize mental health solutions through Extended Reality (XR) technologies.
